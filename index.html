The whole thesis in one line

One thread: the chemical bond

Most of materials science treats strength, slip, and phase stability as separate problems. I kept finding that if you look closely enough at the bonds between atoms, they are the same problem seen from different distances. Each chapter zooms out one scale.

The journey · five chapters

Climbing the length scales

I started where everything begins — a single atom slotting into a titanium lattice — and ended four years later with an agent that automates the whole pipeline. Here is the climb, scale by scale. Click any stop to read that part of the story.

01
Electronic structure · DFT

How strong is a bond?published

A few parts-per-million of carbon, nitrogen or oxygen can transform titanium. I built Φeff, a parameter-free descriptor from the electronic structure that predicts how much each interstitial strengthens the metal — no fitting to experiment.

Read chapter 1 →
02
Bond anisotropy · DFT + LOBSTER

Which way does a metal slip?published + draft

Strength is a number; slip is a direction. The R* and Γ descriptors read bond anisotropy to predict whether an HCP metal — or alloy — slips on its basal or prismatic planes.

Read chapter 2 →
03
Chemistry at scale · COMPASS

Will two elements even mix?under review

COMPASS turns the chemical bond into thirteen orbital descriptors and classifies how any two of 69 elements behave — isomorphous, partial, intermetallic or immiscible — within ~0.07 macro-F1 of a 132-feature black box, but fully interpretable. Backed by 970 hand-verified pairs and a 127k-entry cross-source DFT database.

Read chapter 3 →
04
Real polycrystal · DFT → MD → DAMASK + experiment

Does it hold up in a real metal?in progress

Here the bonds meet the real world. A hierarchical ICME pipeline carries titanium from electrons (VASP) through atoms (LAMMPS) to a deforming polycrystal (DAMASK), checked against my own cryogenic tension tests and EBSD down to 143 K.

Read chapter 4 →
05
Automation · multi-agent ICME

Can an agent do what took me four years?work in progress

The culmination: a multi-agent framework where you describe a calculation in plain English and VASP, LAMMPS and DAMASK agents set it up, run it on the cluster, and pass results down the chain. The VASP agent already reproduces reference data to within 1%.
